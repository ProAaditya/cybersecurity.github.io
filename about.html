What Is Cyber Security?

Cyber security, often referred to as information technology security or computer security, is the practice of protecting computers,
servers, networks, data, and digital systems from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, and data breaches.
It encompasses a wide range of measures and practices designed to safeguard digital assets and ensure the confidentiality,
integrity, and availability of information and technology resources.


  • The primary goals of cyber security include:

    • 1. **Confidentiality**: Ensuring that sensitive information is only accessible to authorized individuals and not disclosed to unauthorized parties.

    2. **Integrity**: Maintaining the accuracy, consistency, and reliability of data and systems by preventing unauthorized modifications or tampering.

    3. **Availability**: Ensuring that systems and data are accessible and usable by authorized users whenever needed, and preventing disruptions due to attacks or failures.

    4. **Authentication and Authorization**: Verifying the identity of users and granting them appropriate levels of access based on their roles and permissions.

    5. **Non-Repudiation**: Ensuring that actions or transactions carried out by users cannot be denied later, providing a record of activities for accountability.

    6. **Resilience and Recovery**: Developing strategies and plans to recover from cyber attacks and quickly restore systems and data in the event of a breach or incident.

  • Cyber security involves various practices and technologies, including:

    • - **Firewalls and Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)**: These help monitor and control network traffic to prevent unauthorized access and detect potential threats.

    - **Antivirus and Antimalware Software**: These tools scan for and remove malicious software that can compromise the security of systems and data.

    - **Encryption**: The process of converting data into a code to prevent unauthorized access. It's widely used to protect sensitive information during transmission and storage.

    - **Multi-factor Authentication (MFA)**: Requires users to provide multiple forms of verification before accessing a system or data.

    - **Patch Management**: Regularly updating software and systems with the latest security patches to address vulnerabilities and weaknesses.

    - **Security Policies and Training**: Establishing clear security policies and providing training to users to raise awareness and promote good security practices.

    - **Incident Response and Recovery Plans**: Developing strategies to handle and recover from security incidents effectively.

    As technology continues to advance, cyber security becomes increasingly important to protect against a wide range of threats, including hacking,
    phishing, malware, ransomware, and other forms of cyber attacks. It is a critical aspect of both individual and organizational security in today's digital age.
